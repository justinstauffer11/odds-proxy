Betting Guide

What every number means and how the algorithm works

Core Concepts
Implied %
The win probability the bookmaker's odds imply — but it includes the house's cut (vig), so it's always slightly inflated. Example: if a team is priced at −120 American odds, the implied chance is 54.5%. But the true probability is lower because the vig pushes both sides above 50% so the book profits regardless of outcome.
Formula: 1 ÷ decimal odds
Fair %
The true win probability after removing the bookmaker's vig. We take the implied % from each approved book, average them (weighting Pinnacle 2.5× because they're the sharpest), then normalise so the two sides sum to exactly 100%. This is the closest you can get to reality using market data alone.
If Implied Home = 56% and Implied Away = 48% (sum = 104%), Fair Home = 56/104 = 53.8%
EV% — Expected Value
The most important number in this app. EV tells you the average profit per dollar wagered over thousands of bets. Positive EV (+) means the bet is mathematically profitable long-term. Negative EV (−) means the book has an edge over you — don't bet it.
Formula: (Fair% × profit if win) − ((1 − Fair%) × stake lost)
Example: Fair% = 55%, decimal odds = 2.10 → EV = (0.55 × 1.10) − (0.45 × 1) = +15.5%
Decimal Odds
The European format used internally. The number shows your total return per $1 staked — including your original dollar back. So 2.10 means bet $100, get $210 back ($110 profit). American odds (+110 or −120) are converted to decimal automatically.
American +110 → Decimal 2.10  |  American −150 → Decimal 1.67  |  American −110 → Decimal 1.91
Vig / Juice
The bookmaker's built-in commission. It's hidden in the odds — they shorten both sides slightly so the implied probabilities sum above 100%. A typical book runs 4–8% vig; Pinnacle runs just 1–2%, which is why their lines are the most accurate reflection of true probability.
Pinnacle vig: ~1–2%  |  DraftKings/FanDuel: ~5–8%
Algorithm Numbers
Confidence Score (0–100)
A composite score that combines six signals into one number. The higher it is, the more the model, the stats, and the market all agree. Below 55 = not shown on the Home page. Below 70 = shown in Analyzer only. Above 70 = auto-tracked. Above 80 = strong signal. The cap is 88 normally, 93 only when every signal aligns perfectly.
Components: Model edge (45 pts max) + Rating gap (25 pts) + EV score (18 pts) + Streak bonus (3 pts) + Corsi (±8 pts) + PDO (±5 pts)
Rating Gap
The difference between the two teams' internal power ratings (0–1 scale). A gap of 0.10 is the minimum required for a recommendation. Larger gaps mean more dominant edge. The score is capped at 25 points to prevent a single runaway stat from inflating confidence unrealistically.
Gap 0.10 = minimum threshold  |  Gap 0.25+ = significant edge
Model Edge
How much the algorithm's win probability differs from the fair market probability. If the model says a team has a 60% chance and the market says 52%, the edge is 8%. A minimum 5% edge is required before the app will show a recommendation — below that the signal isn't reliable enough.
Required: Model edge ≥ 5% + EV ≥ 3.5% + Rating gap ≥ 0.10 (all three must pass)
Kelly % (¼ Kelly)
The Kelly Criterion is a formula for the mathematically optimal fraction of your bankroll to bet given your edge and the odds. Full Kelly is too aggressive (large swings), so the app uses ¼ Kelly, which is the standard conservative version used by professional bettors. It scales bet size with confidence in the edge.
Formula: Kelly = (Fair% × odds − 1) ÷ (odds − 1)  →  use 25% of that result
CLV — Closing Line Value
Did you get better odds than the line the game closed at? Professional bettors judge their process by CLV, not just wins and losses. Consistently beating the closing line means you're finding real edges. The tracker records the odds at the time you saved the bet so you can track your CLV over time.
You bet Home at 2.10. Game closes at 1.85. Your CLV = +positive — you got the better number.
Hockey-Specific Metrics
Corsi / CF%
Shot attempt share — the percentage of all shot attempts (on goal, blocked, missed) a team generates while at even strength. It measures puck possession and sustained offensive pressure. CF% above 50% means the team dominates possession. Research shows it's the single most predictive NHL metric for future performance. When Corsi disagrees with the model, confidence is penalised by up to 5 points.
CF% 55% = very strong possession team  |  CF% 45% = being outplayed badly
PDO
Shooting percentage + save percentage at even strength, added together. The league average is always 1.000. Teams above 1.020 are likely getting lucky (high PDO regresses down); teams below 0.980 are likely unlucky (low PDO regresses up). The model uses this to fade over-performing teams and back under-performing ones.
PDO 1.030 = hot streak, likely to cool  |  PDO 0.970 = due for improvement
Backup Goalie Signal
When a team starts a backup goalie (identified by a goalie rating below 0.35), the model adds 0.40 goals to the expected total and shows an Over banner. Historically, games where a backup starts see the Over hit at 62.9% — a meaningful edge on totals bets.
Starter out → Over banner appears → historical Over rate: 62.9%
Line Movement
The change in odds from when the app first loaded a game to the current price. If the odds shorten (e.g. 2.10 → 1.90) it means money is coming in on that side — often from sharp bettors who know something. A ▲ badge means the price moved in your favour; ▼ means it moved against you since the app opened.
▲ green = odds improved since opening  |  ▼ red = odds shortened (money on this side)
Baseball-Specific Metrics
ERA (Earned Run Average)
The average number of earned runs a pitching staff allows per 9 innings. Lower is better. MLB average is 4.10. Teams with ERA below 3.50 have elite pitching; above 5.00 is a serious weakness. The model penalises high-ERA teams vs the league average.
ERA 3.10 = elite  |  ERA 4.10 = average  |  ERA 5.20 = below average
WHIP (Walks + Hits per Inning Pitched)
Measures how many baserunners a pitching staff allows per inning. League average ≈ 1.28. Below 1.10 is elite; above 1.45 means pitchers are regularly in trouble. WHIP is a cleaner predictor than ERA because it strips out sequencing luck.
WHIP 1.05 = elite  |  WHIP 1.28 = average  |  WHIP 1.50 = struggling
OPS (On-Base + Slugging)
The single most predictive team offensive stat. Combines on-base percentage (getting on base) and slugging percentage (hitting for power). MLB average is .720. Teams above .780 have elite offenses; below .660 are struggling to score. The model uses OPS as 20% of the power rating.
OPS .800 = elite offense  |  OPS .720 = average  |  OPS .650 = weak
Run Differential / Game
Runs scored minus runs allowed, averaged per game. This is the MLB equivalent of goal differential in hockey — the single best predictor of true team quality. A team winning with a poor run differential is likely getting lucky; a team losing with strong run differential is due for positive regression.
+1.5 R/G diff = genuine contender  |  0.0 = balanced  |  −1.5 = real concern
K/9 (Strikeouts per 9 Innings)
How many batters the pitching staff strikes out per 9 innings. High K/9 (above 9.5) means pitchers are generating weak contact and reducing ball-in-play luck. MLB average is 8.6. A strikeout-heavy staff is more consistent because results depend less on fielding and luck.
K/9 over 10 = elite, very consistent  |  K/9 below 7.5 = relies on defense
Quality Start % (QS%)
Percentage of games where the starter went at least 6 innings allowing 3 or fewer earned runs. High QS% means starters are going deep into games, protecting the bullpen. Teams with low QS% burn their bullpen early, which inflates late-game ERA and creates fatigue over a series.
QS% 55%+ = strong rotation  |  QS% below 35% = bullpen reliance
Tracker & Performance
Algorithm Rating (1–10)
The tracker's overall score for how well the model's recommendations have performed. It's not just win rate — it weights four factors to give a true picture of model quality.
Win vs Expected  40% — Did we win the bets we should have, given fair prob?
EV Calibration  30% — Were positive-EV picks actually profitable?
CLV Score  20% — Did we consistently beat the closing line?
Sample Size  10% — More bets = more reliable rating
AUTO Badge (Purple)
Picks marked AUTO were added to the tracker automatically when the model hit 70+ confidence — you didn't have to save them manually. The app checks ESPN results every time you open the Tracker tab and resolves Won/Lost automatically. This lets you evaluate the model's performance even for games you didn't manually track.
Parlay EV
When two or more games have positive EV, the Parlay Calculator at the bottom of the Analyzer combines them and checks whether the joint bet still beats the book's combined vig. A parlay amplifies both upside and downside — the combined EV must be positive and the odds must be better than just betting each leg separately for it to make sense.
Combined fair prob = Fair% Leg1 × Fair% Leg2  |  Parlay EV uses same formula as single-game EV
